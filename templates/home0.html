Features of the Iris Flower:

  1. Sepal Length (cm)
    • The sepal is the outer part of the flower that protects the petals before it blooms.
    • Length is how long the sepal is, measured in centimeters.
  2. Sepal Width (cm)
    • How wide the sepal is.
    • Together with sepal length, it gives an idea of the flowerâ€™s outer shape.
  3. Petal Length (cm)
    • Petals are the colorful parts that attract pollinators.
    • This is how long the petal is
  4. Petal Width (cm)
    • How thick or wide the petal is

Target (Label):

  • Each sample is classified into one of three species of Iris:
    • Iris setosa
    • Iris versicolor
    • Iris virginica

Why these features?

These measurements were chosen because they vary in predictable ways between species. For example:

  • Iris setosa tends to have shorter, narrower petals, making it easier to distinguish.
  • The other two species are a bit trickier to tell apart.
